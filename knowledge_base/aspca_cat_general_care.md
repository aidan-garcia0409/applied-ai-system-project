# General Cat Care Guide
Source: ASPCA (aspca.org/pet-care/cat-care/general-cat-care)
Species: cat

## Feeding

High-quality, brand-name cat food is recommended, with your veterinarian assessing your specific cat's needs based on age, activity level, and health. Cats require taurine, an essential amino acid, for heart and eye health. The selected food should be appropriately balanced for your cat's life stage. Ensure fresh, clean water is always available, refilling water bowls daily. Treats should comprise no more than 5-10% of your cat's diet.

When introducing baby food to a sick or anorexic cat, carefully check labels to avoid products containing onion or garlic powder, which can be toxic. Contact your veterinarian if digestive issues or lethargy persist beyond two days.

## Grooming

Most cats naturally maintain cleanliness and rarely require baths. Regular brushing or combing helps maintain coat health, reduces shedding, and minimizes hairballs.

## Litter Box Management

All indoor cats need a litter box, which should be placed in a quiet, accessible location. For multi-level homes, one box per floor is recommended. Scoop solid waste daily and perform complete cleanings at least weekly. Avoid ammonia, deodorants, or scented products during cleaning. If your cat refuses the litter box, consult your veterinarian, as this can indicate medical issues.

## Health and Veterinary Care

Annual veterinary examinations and vaccinations are essential. Spay or neuter your cat by five months of age. Seek immediate care for illness or injury.
