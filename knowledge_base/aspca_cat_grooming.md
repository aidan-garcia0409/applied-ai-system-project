# Cat Grooming Guide
Source: ASPCA (aspca.org/pet-care/cat-care/cat-grooming-tips)
Species: cat

## Bathing Your Cat

Baths should be scheduled when cats are relaxed. Before bathing, trim claws for safety and brush out loose hair and mats. Place cotton in ears to prevent water entry, and use three to four inches of lukewarm water in a tub with a rubber mat. When washing, dilute cat shampoo at a 1:5 ratio with water and gently massage from head to tail, avoiding the face. Rinse thoroughly to prevent skin irritation.

## Brushing Your Cat

Regular brushing removes dirt, dead hair, and stimulates blood circulation. One to two sessions weekly benefits most cats, particularly as they age. For short-haired cats, use a metal comb working through the fur in the direction of growth. Long-haired cats require grooming every few days to prevent tangles. Neglecting to brush your cat's coat can lead to painful tangles and a bellyful of hair from self-grooming. During grooming sessions, check for wounds, parasites, and signs of fleas or tapeworms.

## Nail Care

Trim nails every ten to fourteen days. Start by desensitizing your cat to the clippers gradually. Only cut the white part of the claw, avoiding the pink quick where nerves and blood vessels are located. Declawing is strongly discouraged, as it involves amputating the end of a cat's toes. Instead, maintain regular trimming and provide scratching posts.

## Ear Cleaning

Healthy ears are pale pink with minimal wax. Check weekly for debris or discharge. Use a cotton ball with vet-recommended ear cleaner, gently wiping visible debris. Never probe inside the ear canal. Watch for persistent scratching, odor, dark discharge, or head shaking — these may indicate ear mites or infections requiring veterinary attention.

## Dental Care

Brush teeth using cat-formulated toothpaste and a soft-bristled brush designed for felines. Start by massaging gums, then gradually introduce the toothbrush. Chew toys help naturally clean teeth and massage gums. Watch for gingivitis, periodontitis, or mouth ulcers — all warrant immediate veterinary evaluation. Healthy gums are firm and pink, not white or red, with clean teeth free of tartar buildup.

## Eye Care

Healthy eyes are clear and bright with white areas around the eyeballs. Gently wipe away crusty discharge using a fresh cotton ball for each eye, wiping away from the corner. Seek veterinary care if you notice discharge, watering, redness, cloudiness, or if your cat squints or paws at its eyes frequently.
