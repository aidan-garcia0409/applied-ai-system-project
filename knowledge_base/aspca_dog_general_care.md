# General Dog Care Guide
Source: ASPCA (aspca.org/pet-care/dog-care/general-dog-care)
Species: dog

## Feeding Schedule and Nutrition

Puppies require multiple meals throughout their development: four meals daily for puppies eight to twelve weeks old, three meals for those three to six months old, and two meals for puppies six months to one year. When your dog reaches his first birthday, one meal a day is usually enough. However, larger breeds and dogs prone to bloat benefit from two smaller meals instead.

High-quality dry dog food provides balanced nutrition for adult dogs and can be supplemented with water, broth, or canned food. While dogs may enjoy cottage cheese, cooked eggs, fruits, and vegetables, these additions should not total more than ten percent of his daily food intake. Puppies specifically need high-quality puppy formulas, with large-breed puppies requiring specially formulated large-breed puppy foods. Fresh, clean water must remain available constantly, and food and water dishes should be washed frequently.

## Exercise and Mental Stimulation

Dogs require regular physical activity to maintain health and manage weight. Exercise also tends to help dogs avoid boredom, which can lead to destructive behaviors. Exercise needs vary based on breed, age, sex, and overall health. Supervised games and activities naturally satisfy dogs' instincts to dig, chase, retrieve, herd, and chew.

## Grooming and Hygiene

Regular brushing keeps coats clean and reduces shedding. During warm months, daily inspections for fleas and ticks are essential, and a flea comb can help remove parasites. Most dogs need bathing only a few times yearly. Before bathing, remove all mats by combing or cutting, then rinse thoroughly to prevent dirt from sticking to soap residue.

## Housing and Comfort

Dogs need a warm, quiet place to rest, away from all drafts and off the floor. A training crate or dog bed with clean bedding is ideal. Outdoor dogs require shade, cool water during heat, and warm, dry shelter during cold weather.

## Preventive Health Care

Licensing and identification through collars, ID tags, microchips, or tattoos help reunite lost dogs with owners. Spaying and neutering by six months is recommended. Vaccinations provide important health protection. Never give your dog medication that has not been prescribed by a veterinarian.
