# Dog Grooming Guide
Source: ASPCA (aspca.org/pet-care/dog-care/dog-grooming-tips)
Species: dog

## Bathing

The ASPCA recommends bathing dogs at least once every three months, but some may require more frequent baths if they spend a lot of time outdoors or have skin problems. The process involves brushing first, using lukewarm water, and avoiding the ears, eyes, and nose during rinsing. Pet-specific shampoos are essential, as human products may irritate skin.

## Brushing Frequency

Grooming schedules depend on coat type. Dogs with short, smooth coats need weekly brushing. Long-haired breeds demand daily attention to prevent tangles and matting, and long-haired dogs prone to matting benefit from established daily grooming routines. Regular brushing also distributes natural skin oils and keeps the coat healthy.

## Nail Trimming

Nails should be trimmed when they just about touch the ground when the dog walks. For sedentary dogs this may mean weekly trims, while active dogs on rough surfaces can go longer. Two trimmer styles — scissors and guillotine types — work equally well. Overgrown nails can curve into the paw pad, causing pain and infection.

## Ear Care

Regular ear checks are important, especially for dogs producing excessive wax or with heavy inner-ear hair. Using cotton balls dampened with mineral oil or specialized ear cleaner, gently wipe visible debris. Never insert anything into the ear canal, as this risks trauma or infection. Signs of ear problems include head shaking, scratching at ears, and dark or foul-smelling discharge.

## Dental Care

Brushing teeth two to three times weekly helps prevent plaque buildup and tartar. Start by massaging lips, then introduce dog-formulated toothpaste and a soft-bristled brush. Avoid human toothpaste, which can upset a dog's stomach. Dental disease is one of the most common health problems in dogs and is largely preventable with regular brushing.
