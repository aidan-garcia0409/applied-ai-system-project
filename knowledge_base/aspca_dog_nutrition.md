# Dog Nutrition Guide
Source: ASPCA (aspca.org/pet-care/dog-care/dog-nutrition-tips)
Species: dog

## Essential Nutrients

Dogs require six nutrient classes for optimal health. Water accounts for between 60 to 70% of an adult pet's body weight and must be available continuously. A 10% decrease in body water causes serious illness, while 15% loss can be fatal.

Proteins serve as building blocks for cells and tissues, sourced from meats like chicken and fish (complete proteins) or vegetables and grains (incomplete proteins). Raw eggs should be avoided, as they contain avidin, which interferes with nutrient metabolism.

Fats provide concentrated energy — more than twice that of proteins or carbohydrates — and support hormone production and vitamin absorption. Carbohydrates supply energy and support intestinal health. Moderately fermentable fibers like beet pulp benefit digestive health without causing excess gas. Vitamins and minerals cannot be synthesized by dogs and must come from food; supplementation should only occur when deficiencies are diagnosed.

## Feeding Schedules

The ASPCA recommends all dogs be fed twice daily, spaced eight to twelve hours apart. Three feeding methods exist:

- Portion-control: Measured meals for weight management
- Free-choice: Food available constantly (best for dry food only)
- Timed feeding: Food available for set periods only

## Puppies

Weaning typically begins at three to four weeks and completes by seven to eight weeks. Puppies need high-quality puppy food with 25-30% protein and up to twice the energy intake of adult dogs. Overfeeding risks rapid growth and joint problems, particularly in large breeds.

## Adult Dogs

Feeding amounts depend on size and activity level. Active dogs may require maintenance plus 20 to 40% additional energy. Treats should be given in moderation and represent five percent or less of the dog's daily food intake.

## Senior Dogs

Older dogs need consistent protein levels to maintain muscle mass. Senior dietary considerations typically begin between five to seven years old depending on breed size.

## Weight Management

Signs of obesity include inability to feel ribs and lack of visible waist definition. Management involves dietary correction, increased exercise, and behavioral modifications. Removing pets from the room during family meals reduces begging behavior.
