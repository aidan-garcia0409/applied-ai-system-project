# Dog Exercise and Daily Walks
Source: Curated from ASPCA, AKC, and AVMA published guidelines
Species: dog

## Daily Exercise Requirements

Most adult dogs need at least 30 minutes to 2 hours of exercise per day, depending on their energy level and age. At minimum, dogs should receive two walks per day — one in the morning and one in the evening. Skipping daily walks leads to weight gain, behavioral problems, and reduced cardiovascular health.

High-energy breeds (retrievers, herding dogs, terriers) require 1-2 hours of vigorous activity. Lower-energy breeds and senior dogs may do well with two 20-30 minute walks daily. Puppies should not be over-exercised; a common guideline is 5 minutes of exercise per month of age, twice daily, until growth plates close.

## Why Walks Matter Beyond Physical Health

Walks provide essential mental stimulation — smells, sights, and sounds engage a dog's brain as much as their body. Dogs deprived of regular walks commonly develop anxiety, destructive chewing, excessive barking, and house-soiling behaviors. A dog that receives adequate daily exercise is calmer, easier to train, and less likely to develop stress-related behaviors.

## Morning Walks

A morning walk is especially important because dogs have been resting for 7-9 hours overnight. Morning exercise helps regulate bowel movements, reduces the risk of accidents indoors, and sets a calm behavioral baseline for the day. Veterinarians recommend morning walks as the single highest-priority daily task for dog owners.

## Evening Walks

An evening walk completes the daily activity cycle, releases remaining energy built up during the day, and gives the dog an opportunity to eliminate before bedtime. Evening walks reduce nighttime restlessness and help dogs sleep through the night.

## Water During and After Exercise

Dogs should have access to fresh water before and after walks. In warm weather, carry water on walks longer than 20 minutes. Overheating is a serious risk, particularly for brachycephalic breeds (bulldogs, pugs, boxers). Signs of overheating include excessive panting, drooling, and stumbling.

## Exercise and Weight Management

Regular walking is the most effective tool for maintaining healthy body weight in dogs. A 30-minute brisk walk burns approximately 100-140 calories for a medium-sized dog. Combined with portion-controlled feeding, daily walks prevent obesity, which is the leading preventable health problem in companion dogs.
